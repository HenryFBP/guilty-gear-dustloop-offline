Read First

  • All damage values were tested against Ky at full life with the R.I.S.C Level at 0.

Fire Notation

  • ]H[ refers to Fire.
    • Technically, Fire can only be input by releasing . This is denoted by ]H[, but for the most part it can be thought of as a regular H.
  • ]H[* explicitly refers to performing Fire with an accurate reticle.
    • Fire deals more damage if you give the aiming reticle time to focus down on the opponent and in Steady Aim it gives a higher launch on hit as well. Assume the combos listed here work with either type of shot unless stated otherwise in the move notes or with ]H[*.
  • [H] refers to entering At the Ready stance. For the most part, Chaos can enter stance at any point, but some combos may have a suggested point for entering stance.
  • 2H Always refers to exiting At the Ready. Crouching Fire may be notated as 2]H[, but also 1]H[ or 3]H[

Other Notation

  • 22P (N) - Refers to reloading exactly N bullets (e.g. 22P (1) notates canceling reload right after the first bullet is loaded).

Combo Theory

Charged 214S

A fully aimed (or charged) shot (]H[*) from 214S is incredibly powerful, both on hit or block. The bonus damage and higher launch allows for much greater combo potental, upgrading a 20% combo to something akin to 50% with wall carry or even wall break. On block, the guard crush cancelled into 22P(1) is very plus on block, allowing for extended pressure. Needless to say, being able to execute this move is a very powerful tool in Happy Chao's arsenal.

The problem is that linking a charged shot from a non-counterhit normal is impossible. The only way to combo/string into it safely is with ]H[ from At the Ready. Accomplishing this is very, very difficult. The whole thing (214]H[S ~ ]H[* > 22P(1) > 66, ...) requires 2 frame-perfect inputs. The first 1-frame link is between using Fire (]H[), then immediately entering Steady Aim (214S) on the next frame. The second 1-frame window occurs when timing the final Fire ]H[* shot so that it charges its full duration, but not charging it for too long that the combo drops.

However, there are some saving graces with both links. For the first; you will notice that combos with this technique have adjusted notation. ]H[ and 214S have been combined together to create 214]H[S. Performing this input as written uses the negative edge properties of Fire to make the link as consistent as possible. Essentially, you can treat this as releasing at almost exactly the same time as you press . When doing this technique, if you enter Steady Aim without using Fire, then you held for an extra frame or two.

For the second link, the combo will still work if you use an uncharged ]H[, however the knockup will not be substatial enough to continue the combo without spending meter or starting from point-blanc range. Essentially, if you aren't confident in your ability to land the links you can always fire before the shot is charged for a decent damage and knockback. This knockback can be used to cancel into whichever resource special you want (214P, 22P, or 236P.

If you are playing on pad, these kind of inputs are notoriously difficult if both and are mapped to the face buttons, as you will be attempting to use one finger to seamlessly hold and transfer to another button in 1 frame. If you intend to practice this technique, it is recommended that you remap one of the buttons to a bumper or trigger at the back of your controller, where another finger can independantly press them.
