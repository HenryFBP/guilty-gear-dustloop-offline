Combo Theory

The general goal is to use 236S in combos as many times as possible as it does high damage, has good stage carry, and can be followed up by additional air actions or j.D to convert into wall bounces/wall breaks when the positioning is correct. Ending a combo with 236S also creates a hard knockdown to set up 236P. Using 236[S] multiple times in a combo is known as a "Kou loop", and is possible off some mid-screen hits and in many corner combo situations.

How to combo into Kou is not always consistent, but these sequences are ideas for how to do so:

  • c.S > 2H > 236[S] (used off high juggles like 2H or red RC)
  • c.S > 236S (may allow for an additional 236S later in the combo or provide better scaling for another 236S through omitting the 2H)
  • 2K > 236S (may allow for combo-ing into 236S even if the opponent falls low to the ground)
  • 236[S] or 236S on its own earlier in the combo may allow for additional 236S later in the combo

When attempting to convert into a wall bounce, it is best to delay j.D as much as possible to increase the consistency of being able to follow up on it. Ideally, the opponent should hit by the top half (or higher) of Anji's umbrella. From there, 5K, f.S, or c.S into 236H->H will normally wall splat if j.D was the first wall hit. 5K is the most consistent, but lowest damage of these follow-ups.

After a wall splat, 632146H is the obvious candidate for the most damage, but 5H, 2H, and 5D are the highest damage non-metered moves to wall break from a grounded position so use whichever is the most comfortable/intuitive for you.

After an opponent is slowed down by a Blue RC, any single hit will provide enough hit stun to go into a c.S / f.S > 2H > 236[S] or fully charged 236[H]->K into any juggle combo. Use whichever is appropriate for the situation - the former is generally better for damage but the latter is generally better for stage carry or when you cannot connect a move into 2H.
